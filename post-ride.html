Notes from a Morning Ride

April 10, 2026 • Journal

T

here is a specific kind of quiet that only exists early in the morning on rural paths, right before the sun fully burns off the mist. The air is cool, and the only sound is the rhythm of bicycle tires crunching against the dirt road.

Taking these group cycling routes has become a grounding practice. It forces you to look up and simply pay attention to the changing light over the fields.

Rest stop along the rural trails.

By the time we looped back, the sun was high and the quiet was gone, replaced by the usual daily noise. But keeping that morning stillness with you changes the texture of the rest of the day.
